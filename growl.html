Introduction

Brief

Redesign the GROWL! website to better reflect the character and atmosphere of GROWL! and provide new features to help the business owner attract new customers while maintaining current branding and logo.

Goals

1. Design an updated, responsive website that is user friendly and on brand.

2. Incorporate best practices for SEO.

Overview

Role: Project manager and sole end-to-end UX/UI designer

Timeline: 2 weeks

Research

Research Goals

We want to...

  1. identify user pain points with the current website
  2. understand user motivations and needs when using the website
  3. identify a clear brand message for the business
  4. identify best practices for websites for local businesses looking to increase customer base, including SEO optimization

in order to...

create an effective, user-friendly responsive website that attracts new customers through communicating a specific brand message and meeting users’ needs.

Competitive Analysis

My analysis of local bars, brewries, and restaurants in competition with GROWL! showed that strong local business websites should:

  1. have strong branding
  2. have plentiful content, especially images
  3. have clear top navigation
  4. have an effective responsive site design
  5. avoiding loading and accessibility issues

User Research

I created an 11-question survey based on the project brief, research goals, and competitive analysis. The survey was targeted to current GROWL! customers and advertised on flyers in the bar and on social media.

In the first section, participants were asked demographic questions to help identify GROWLS!’s main customer bases. In the second section, participants were asked questions about their patronage of GROWL! Finally, participants were asked questions about the GROWL! website.

The 32 respondents shared many similar characteristics and opinions about GROWL! and its website.

How They Decribed GROWL!

  • Good beer
  • Cozy neighborhood bar
  • Friendly, chill, positive

How They Described the Ideal GROWL! Website

  • Multipage design instead of scrolling one page
  • Simple design
  • Convey the character of GROWL! more through its design, content, and images
  • Have a hierarchy that focuses on providing key information and conveying the character of GROWL!

Define

Persona

Three personas were created to represent the different age ranges and frequency of GROWL! patronage of the survey participants. The survey data showed that patrons liked GROWL! because of its friendly and relaxed atmosphere. They appreciated the lack of loud music, the unique features such as the art displays and record player, and the friendliness of the staff and other patrons.

Product Roadmap

I created a product roadmap to list, describe, and prioritize the features of the updated GROWL! site. Based on my survey data, I prioritized features that customers wanted to see on the site such as a live beer list. The second ranked priorities included features that would allow the business owner to regularly update the site, such as a news feature, and that would help attract new customers, such as reviews.

Testing

Usability Testing

To test the structure and organization of the redesign, I conducted remote usability tests using the Invision prototype and Maze. The tests focused on three tasks that new or returning users might want to accomplish.

Scenario 1

1. Subscribe to the email mailing list.

Scenario 2

1. Search for information about happy hour.

Scenario 3

1. Use the main navigation menu to find information about the wine offerings.


Users were able to complete all three scenarios successfully, although scenario 1 proved to be somewhat challenging.

Scenario 1

  • Completion rate: 64.3%
  • Misclick rate: 36%

Scenario 2

  • Completion rate: 83.3%
  • Misclisk rate: 16%

Scenario 3

  • Completion rate: 100%
  • Misclisk rate: 9%

Affinity Map

I created an affinity map to help with my analysis of the usability testing results. The map highlighted test participants’ comments about the ease of using and navigating the redesigned site and helped me draw some conclusions about the misclick rates in the three scenarios.

Results

Based on the results of the usability testing, I determined that the redesigned site had established a clear visual hierarchy, was easy to use, and strongly conveyed the brand identity. Analyzing the test results demonstrated some issues with the set up of usability tests, including the need for clearer instructions for some of the tasks and the need to anticipate more potential paths through each task.

Follow the usability tests, I made some changes to the layout and design of the pages as I moved from wireframes to UI designs, such as reducing the size of the hero images—an issues noted by test participants—to bring more content above the fold and encourage users to scroll. I also made some changes to the designs based on feedback from the business owner and developer, such as removing the search function. Neither thought it was necessary since the overall structure and organization of the site were clear, simple, and easy to navigate.

Conclusion

Summary

Working on this project provided a great opportunity to learn about best practices for local business websites and SEO and to focus on information architecture and visual design. My previous experience as a project manager helped me fulfill my dual roles on the project and communicate effectively with the business owner and developer.

I learned a lot about balancing the needs of different stakeholders on a UX project and challenges of creating a fresh design within the boundaries of an existing brand style with a defined color palette.

Next Steps

This project is currently in development, with the redesigned site scheduled to go live sometime in September 2019. The final steps needed before that can happen are:

  1. Obtain outstanding content from the business owner and incorporate into the UI designs.
  2. Turnover final content to the developer to go live.

Phase 2 of the project will include designing and developing an admin portal for the business owner to use to update certain content on the site. If the project continues beyond phase 2, potential next steps include:

  1. Conducting another round usability testing using the UI designs and additional scenarios and adjusting the designs and features as needed.
  2. Building out additional content pages that go more in-depth into some of the unique features of the bar, such as providing a library of games and records available on site.
  3. Research into rebranding.
